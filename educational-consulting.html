Educational Consulting

Schools and universities are navigating one of the most demanding periods in the history of education. Expectations are rising, resources are stretched, and the pace of change, particularly around artificial intelligence and instructional technology, has outrun many institutions' ability to respond thoughtfully. Meanwhile, the most important variable in student success remains the same as it has always been: the quality of teaching.

Olive Tree Consultants works with schools and institutions to strengthen the teaching, learning, and instructional practice that make the biggest difference, not through prepackaged programs, but through engagements designed around your people, your priorities, and your context.

What We Do

We partner with educational leaders to design and deliver professional development that faculty and teachers actually engage with, because it reflects their real challenges, fits their environment, and builds skills they can apply immediately.

  • Teacher and faculty development: Building practical instructional skills, collaborative learning communities, and a culture of continuous improvement
  • Course and curriculum design: Helping educators design learning experiences that are intentional, effective, and aligned with what students actually need
  • AI and instructional technology integration: Moving beyond novelty and anxiety toward purposeful, pedagogically sound use of AI and technology in teaching and learning
  • Needs assessment and program evaluation: Identifying where development can have the greatest impact and measuring whether it did

Our Approach

We start by listening. Every engagement begins with a needs assessment process designed to understand your institution's specific challenges, your educators' real development needs, and the outcomes that matter most to your leadership. From there, we build: designing programs, workshops, and development experiences from the ground up rather than adapting someone else's curriculum to fit your context.

Our team brings deep expertise in instructional design, faculty and teacher development, educational technology, and assessment. We have worked in and alongside educational institutions for a combined several decades, and we understand both the promise and the practical constraints of doing this work well in real schools and universities.

What to Expect

  • Teachers and faculty engage more deeply because development is relevant, practical, and worth their time
  • Instructional practice improves in ways students notice and leaders can point to
  • Institutions and schools develop a clearer, more confident approach to AI and technology in the classroom
  • Professional development builds internal capacity rather than creating dependency on outside support
  • You gain a clear picture of where teaching is strong, where it is developing, and what the next steps should be
